I trained an ML model on 338,000 CVEs. CVSS score ranked 5th in feature importance for predicting real-world exploitation.

The formula everyone uses for prioritization is the fifth most important feature.

Key findings:

- ML achieves 0.903 AUC vs CVSS threshold at 0.662 — a +24pp improvement
- #1 predictor: vendor CVE count (proxy for deployment ubiquity). Attackers invest where the payoff is highest
- #2 predictor: CVE age. Weaponization takes months, not days
- Adversarial robustness: 0% evasion. Top features (vendor history, age, EPSS) are all outside attacker control
- EPSS (already ML-based) achieves 0.912 AUC — if you're using it, you're ahead of most teams

Stop prioritizing by CVSS alone. Vendor deployment ubiquity and vulnerability age are stronger signals.
