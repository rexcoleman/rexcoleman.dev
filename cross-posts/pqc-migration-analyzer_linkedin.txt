But the real finding: 70% of the crypto in your codebase isn't yours to change.

Key findings:

- 21,142 crypto CVEs in the NVD (6.3% of all CVEs involve cryptographic primitives)
- ML beats rule-based priority scoring by +14pp. Gradient Boosting wins
- Classical exploitability (heap overflows, padding oracles) predicts migration priority better than quantum risk
- 70% library-controlled, 20% developer-controlled, 8% protocol-controlled, 2% hardware-controlled
- 4th domain validation of controllability analysis — same principle, different security context

Fix the padding oracles before the RSA key exchange. Start with the 20% you control.
