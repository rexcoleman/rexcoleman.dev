I sent 19 attack scenarios at a default-configured LangChain ReAct agent. 13 succeeded.

The most dangerous attack — reasoning chain hijacking — hit 100% success rate. And it partially evades every defense I built.

Key findings:

- 7 attack classes systematized — 5 not in OWASP LLM Top 10 or MITRE ATLAS
- Reasoning chain hijacking exploits the agent's core capability (following structured plans) as the attack vector
- Layered defense reduces average attack success by 60%, but reasoning hijack only drops from 100% to 67%
- Attack success correlates inversely with defender observability — the reasoning chain is invisible to defenders
- Claude specifically resists indirect injection via tool outputs (25% success)

The reasoning loop is the feature AND the attack surface.
