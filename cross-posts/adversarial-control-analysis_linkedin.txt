I applied the same security principle to 6 different domains. Every time, the finding was the same.

Adversarial Control Analysis: classify every input by who controls it, then build defenses around what the attacker cannot manipulate.

Results across 6 domains:

- Network IDS: 57/78 features attacker-controlled. Defender-observable features achieve 100% detection on constrained attacks
- Vulnerability prediction: ML beats CVSS by +24pp AUC. Top features are all outside attacker control (0% evasion)
- AI agents: Attack success correlates inversely with defender observability. Reasoning chain (invisible) = 100% attack success
- PQC migration: 70% of your crypto is in libraries you don't control. Start with the 20% you can change
- Fraud detection: System-only features retain 81% detection even if the fraudster controls everything else
- AI supply chain: Defenders control the deserialization pipeline, even though attackers control model files

One principle. Six domains. Same result.
