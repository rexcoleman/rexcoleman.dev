After 14 manual audit cycles with 30+ findings each, I stopped auditing ML projects by hand and built a framework instead.

govML: governance-as-code for ML research.

Key findings:

- 42 templates, 19 generators, 4 quickstart profiles, agent orchestrator prototype
- Adding automated audit generators (G13-G16) dropped manual audit cycles from 14 to zero
- The highest-leverage template was the last one built: PUBLICATION_PIPELINE — govML governed everything except publishing for months
- PROJECT_BRIEF (thesis-first thinking) changed how projects start: one project went from mkdir to complete FINDINGS.md in a single session
- Tested across 9 projects with 469+ tests

Governance docs that aren't executable are decoration. The generators are what actually prevent errors.
