I spent 15 years in cybersecurity — from threat intelligence to security architecture at FireEye and Mandiant. I've seen how organizations defend against nation-state adversaries and how security programs break under pressure.

Now I'm pointing that experience at AI systems.

I'm completing my MS in Computer Science at Georgia Tech (Machine Learning specialization) while building tools that bridge the gap between AI capability and AI security. My current focus:

**AI Security Architecture** — adversarial evaluation of ML systems, building AI-powered security tools, proving they're robust against the attacks I've spent a career studying.

**ML Systems Governance** — [govML](https://github.com/rexcoleman/govML), an open-source framework (32 templates, 7 profiles, 7 generators) that enforces reproducibility and decision traceability across ML projects.

**Adversarial Control Analysis** — a methodology for designing ML systems where the inputs that drive decisions are outside adversary influence. Validated across network intrusion detection and vulnerability prediction. The principle: don't bolt on robustness — architect it in.
